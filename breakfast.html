Breakfast

The meal eaten in the morning. The English term refers to breaking the overnight fasting period. Traditions of eating morning meals have existed since ancient times, though it wasn't until the 15th century that breakfast became used in written English. In medieval Europe, breakfast was considered a luxury for the rich, only a necessity for laborers, or most often skipped. American colonists would eat breakfast but only after a few hours of morning work. Historians agree that breakfast became routine in the morning before work when workers moved to cities and became employees with set schedules.
