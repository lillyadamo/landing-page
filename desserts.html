Dessert

The course that concludes a meal and often consists of sweet foods and possibly a beverage, like liqueur and dessert wine. Though in many parts of the world, like China, West Africa, and many parts of Greece, there is no tradition of dessert concluding a meal. The word derives from the French word: desservir, which means "to clear the table". The table would be cleared and changed for the final course, which was called "le fruit". After the French revolution, the aristrocratic "fruit" was fully replaced by "dessert".
