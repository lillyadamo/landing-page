English Pancakes

For those in North America, English pancakes resemble crepes.

Ingredients

  • 105g plain flour
  • 1 pinch of salt
  • 240 ml milk
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 tablespoon of butter (for cooking)

Instructions

  1. Sift together the flour and salt into a bowl. Add the milk and eggs and whisk well. The batter should flow but not be overly thin. Set aside for a few minutes.
  2. Warm a wide skillet/frying pan or crepe pan overa medium-high heat. Melt a little butter and spread around the pan
  3. Reduce the heat slightly and add around 1/4 cup of the pancake batter to the pan. Tilt the pan slightly as you add the batter and swirl a little so the batter spreads out a bit.
  4. Let the pancake cook until the edges start to curl up and the top is dry - a couple of minutes. If you lift up the edge, you should see a slight browning on the bottom. Flip the pancakeover and cook a minute more on the other side.
  5. Remove the pancake from the pan once the other side gets a few brown spots and keep warm while you cook the rest.
  6. For a traditional topping, sprinkle the pancake with sugar and squeeze over some lemon juice!
