Lunch

The meal eaten around midday. From the Roman era to the middle ages, lunch was called dinner and was the main meal of the day. In the middle ages, people woke up earlier and worked about six hours before getting hungry and stopping for a break. As we became more techologically advanced and artificial light was introduced to us, dinner began to shift later in the day, meaning that a lighter meal in between was needed. Lunch was a rare word until the 19th century.
